My writing envionrment

by Matthew Hendricks

2019-02-20

Google Keep

Everything I write starts as a note, and it's nice to see the archive of old ideas as you look back on a project's process (typos and all).  

Confluence

In my time managing support and product teams, Atlassian products came up everywhere you turn.  Even though I'd first been interested in Atlassian for their issue tracking took JIRA, I was pleasantly surprised when I discovered Confluence – which you can think of as a more pleasant looking Wiki – it doubles as a very incredible knowledge and collaboration tool that rivals Google Docs and also integrates with many common services (not to mention a whole ecosystem of add-ons) .  When I took to starting my consulting services, I instantly knew that I was going to be using Confluence to help organize and plan my content.

Grammarly

I used to shy away from tools that claimed to be grammar-aware but blown away by Grammarly, especially when it comes to its simple double-click synonyms and vocabulary enhancements.

Whether it's working on those quick pre-coffee emails or double-checking comments in source code – it seems I've slowly found a way to work Grammarly into all of my projects.  

Without overlooking their Chrome extension and Mobile Keyboard  –  their web app is simple enough to keep as an open tab and has an intuitive, minimalist UI for sorting documents.  

Ghost blog engine

I'm using Ghost to publish this portfolio/site, and also use it host blog.thoughtaboutsolutions.com content

I stumbled my way around WordPress just enough to get frustrated with it – Ghost was a refreshing change and have found it's JavaScript/Handlebars layout functions much more comfortable than brushing off the old php books.

Jira/Trello

I used to shift back and forth between two, so I was excited to see purchase Trello.  Since then entirely switched to JIRA Core for all of my business projects – JIRA is no longer just for software and IT teams, you can use JIRA to drive billing processes, create sales hooks, and even use it as a fully-fledged customer portal with knowledge base and support contact forms.
